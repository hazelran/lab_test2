Our Research

VCP-Lab investigates video, image, and media technologies aligned with international standards. With an eye toward social and technological changes, we are actively exploring advanced media options such as 4K/8K/16K videos and VR/AR realistic videos, in order to anticipate the future of highly immersive media.

Our research spans the full pipeline from standard algorithm design to AI-based enhancement, combining methodologies from deep learning, signal processing, computer vision, and international standardization (MPEG, JVET).

Video Coding Standards

We actively contribute to international video coding standardization through JVET (Joint Video Experts Team) and MPEG (ISO/IEC JTC1/SC29). Our work spans VVC (H.266), HEVC (H.265), AVC (H.264), AV1, and EVC — developing faster algorithms, lower-complexity implementations, and novel coding tools for adoption in international standards.

Prof. Choi served as Chairman of the Ad Hoc Group on the Essential Video Coding (EVC) standard and received the ISO/IEC Excellence Award (2022) in recognition of outstanding service to ISO/IEC JTC1/SC29.

Team members: Prof. Aiahn Kiho Choi, VCP-Lab members

AI-based Video Enhancement

We develop deep learning models — including CNN, GAN, and Transformer architectures — to enhance the quality of compressed video and images as in-loop or post-processing filters. Our work focuses on multi-scale attention mechanisms, residual learning, and perceptual quality optimization for major codecs including VVC, HEVC, AV1, and EVC.

Team members: Tanni, Xilong, Junghyun, Zezhen Gai, Zhao Bopu, Prof. Choi

Video Coding for Machines (VCM)

As machine vision becomes pervasive in autonomous systems and smart infrastructure, we study how to compress visual data optimized for downstream AI tasks rather than human perception. This includes contributions to the MPEG FCVCM (Feature Coding for Video and Image Coding for Machines) standard.

Our research explores the intersection of traditional video coding and machine learning, investigating efficient feature representations that preserve task-relevant information for detection, recognition, and segmentation.

Team members: Xilong Liang, Prof. Choi

Immersive & Next-Generation Media

We look ahead to 4K/8K/16K ultra-high-resolution video, virtual reality (VR), augmented reality (AR), and 360° omnidirectional video — developing compression and processing technologies to support the next generation of immersive media experiences. This includes our IITP-funded project on next-generation media compression for Metaverse content transmission and storage.

Team members: VCP-Lab members, Prof. Choi
